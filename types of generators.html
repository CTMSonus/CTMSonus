White Noise Generator

This generator emits white noise, which gathers all the frequencies that the human ear is able to pick up, and all of them with the same amplitude and power.

  • (1) Button that allows to mute the whole generator.
  • (2) Label showing the level in decibels of both ears independently.
  • (3) Button that allows to mute the left or right ear.
  • (4) Text field where you can enter numerically the amount of DBSPL you want to emit, and confirm with button (9).
  • (5) Button Button to confirm the change of level set in the text field (8)
  • (6) Button to link the levels of both left and right ears, when they are controlled with buttons (7) and (8).
  • (7) Button to decrease the level of the corresponding side of the generator.
  • (8) Button for increasing the level of the corresponding side of the generator.

Bandpass Generator


This generator outputs white noise filtered to obtain only a portion of the spectrum. The bandwidth is set to Q, centered on the frequency of the generator, with a ratio of 1/30. That is if we set a frequency of 1000, and a bandwidth of 1 we will obtain filtered sound with predominance from 970 hz to 1030 hz.

  • (1) Button that allows to mute the whole generator.
  • (2) Label showing the current frequency of the generator.
  • (3) Label showing the level in decibels for left ear
  • (4) Label showing the level in right ear decibels
  • (5) Slider to move the frequency of the sound emitted by the generator (6) Text field to move the frequency of the sound emitted by the generator
  • (6) Text field where you can enter the frequency in numerical form and press button(6) to set the current frequency of the generator.
  • (7) Button to confirm the change of frequency set in the text field(5)
  • (8) Slider to move the bandwidth in hz for the sound emitted by the generator.
  • (9) Text field where you can enter the bandwidth numerically and pressing button(9) the width in Q
  • (10) Button to confirm the change of bandwidth set in the text field(8)
  • (11) Label showing the actual volume measured for the ears
  • (12) Button to compensate the actual measured volume to the entered target volume
  • (13) Button that allows to mute the left or right ears.
  • (14) Text field where you can enter in numerical form the amount of DBSPL to be emitted, and confirm with button (12).
